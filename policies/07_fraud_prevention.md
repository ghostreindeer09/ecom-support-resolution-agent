# Fraud Prevention Policy

**Document ID:** POL-FRAUD-007
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All transactions and customer interactions on the platform

---

## Section 1: Fraud Detection Signals

The platform monitors for the following fraud indicators across customer interactions:

**Order-level signals:**
1. Multiple orders to the same address with different payment methods
2. Orders with billing and shipping addresses in different countries
3. High-value orders placed by new accounts (created within the last 7 days)
4. Orders using prepaid debit cards or virtual card numbers for high-value items
5. Rapid successive orders within a short time frame (5+ orders in 24 hours)

**Refund and return signals:**
1. Claiming non-delivery despite carrier confirmation with signature — **primary fraud signal**
2. Requesting refund AND retention of the item, especially for non-perishable goods
3. Returning items that appear to be different from what was shipped (return substitution)
4. Pattern of "defective" claims across multiple orders with no photographic evidence
5. Multiple "item not received" claims at the same address within 6 months

**Account-level signals:**
1. Account takeover indicators: sudden change of email, password, and payment method
2. Multiple accounts linked to the same payment method or physical address
3. Rapid escalation through support channels demanding immediate refund

---

## Section 2: Fraud Signal Handling

When a fraud signal is detected during a support interaction:

1. **The support resolution must NOT approve a refund or favorable decision** — the case must be escalated to the fraud investigation team
2. The customer should receive a neutral response indicating that their case requires additional review
3. The fraud investigation team will review the case within **1–2 business days**
4. During the investigation, the customer's account remains active but the disputed transaction is placed on hold

**Important**: A fraud signal does not mean fraud has occurred. It means the case requires human review before any resolution is provided. Automated systems should never deny a claim based solely on fraud signals — they should escalate for human investigation.

---

## Section 3: Customer Communication During Fraud Investigation

When communicating with a customer whose case has been flagged for fraud review:

1. Do NOT inform the customer that their case has been flagged for fraud
2. Use neutral language: "Your case requires additional review by our specialist team"
3. Provide an expected timeline: "You will hear back within 1–2 business days"
4. Do not make any commitments about the outcome
5. Do not ask the customer leading questions about fraud (e.g., "Did someone else use your account?")
6. Document all customer statements in the case notes for the fraud team's review

---

## Section 4: Investigation Outcomes

The fraud investigation team will reach one of the following conclusions:

1. **Cleared — no fraud detected**: The original claim is processed according to standard policy. An apology for the delay may be appropriate.
2. **Suspicious — insufficient evidence**: The claim is processed but the account is flagged for monitoring. Future claims will be subject to additional scrutiny.
3. **Confirmed fraud**: The claim is denied, the customer is notified, and the account may be restricted or terminated. If applicable, the case is referred to law enforcement.
4. **Inconclusive**: The claim is processed as a goodwill gesture but the account is flagged. This outcome is used when fraud indicators are present but cannot be confirmed.

---

## Section 5: Account Restrictions

Accounts confirmed for fraudulent activity may be subject to:

1. Temporary suspension pending investigation
2. Permanent ban with forfeiture of store credit and pending refunds
3. Restriction to specific payment methods (no prepaid cards, no Buy Now/Pay Later)
4. Requirement for photo ID verification for future orders
5. Referral to law enforcement if the fraud amount exceeds $500

Account restrictions are applied by the fraud investigation team only, never by frontline support agents or automated systems.
