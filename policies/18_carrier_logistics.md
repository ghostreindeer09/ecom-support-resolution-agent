# Carrier and Logistics Partner Policies

**Document ID:** POL-CARRIER-018
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All shipments handled by contracted carriers

---

## Section 1: Contracted Carriers

The platform works with the following carrier partners for domestic and international shipments:

**Domestic (US):**
- USPS (United States Postal Service) — standard, priority, and express mail
- UPS (United Parcel Service) — ground, 2-day air, next-day air
- FedEx — home delivery, express, ground
- Amazon Logistics — same-day and next-day delivery in select areas
- Regional carriers — for oversized items and furniture

**International:**
- DHL Express — primary international carrier
- FedEx International — secondary international carrier
- USPS International — economy international shipments

The carrier used for a specific order is determined by the shipping method selected at checkout, the item's dimensions and weight, and the delivery destination. The customer does not select the specific carrier unless they choose a carrier-specific shipping option at checkout.

## Section 2: Carrier Delivery Confirmation Standards

Each carrier provides delivery confirmation through their tracking system. The platform accepts the following as valid delivery confirmation:

### 2.1 Standard Delivery Confirmation
- Status: "Delivered" with date and time stamp
- Location indicator: "Front Door," "Mailbox," "Reception," "Neighbor"
- This is the most common delivery confirmation type and is accepted as proof of delivery

### 2.2 Signature Confirmation
- Status: "Delivered — Signed for by [name]"
- The carrier collected a signature from the recipient or a person at the delivery address
- Signature confirmation is required for all orders above $300 and all electronics orders above $150
- If the delivery requires signature confirmation and no one is available to sign, the carrier will make up to 3 delivery attempts before holding the package for pickup

### 2.3 Photo Confirmation
- Some carriers provide a photograph of the package at the delivery location
- Photo confirmation showing the package at the correct address is treated as strong delivery evidence
- Photo confirmation is not available for all carriers or all delivery locations

### 2.4 GPS Confirmation
- Carrier's delivery device records GPS coordinates at the time of delivery
- GPS confirmation within 50 meters of the delivery address is treated as strong delivery evidence
- GPS confirmation is used primarily in delivery dispute investigations

## Section 3: Carrier Liability

**For lost packages:**
- If a package is confirmed lost by the carrier, the carrier's insurance covers the replacement cost up to the declared value
- The platform handles the customer-facing resolution (refund or replacement) and files a reimbursement claim with the carrier separately
- The customer does not need to interact with the carrier for lost package claims — the platform manages the process

**For damaged packages:**
- If damage is attributed to carrier handling, the platform processes the refund or replacement for the customer and files a damage claim with the carrier
- For high-value items (above $500), the carrier may require an inspection before the claim is processed — the platform will coordinate this with the customer

**For delivery errors:**
- If the carrier delivers to the wrong address, the platform will file a mis-delivery claim with the carrier
- The customer receives a full refund or reshipment regardless of the carrier claim outcome
- If the original item is recovered by the carrier, it will be returned to the fulfillment center

## Section 4: Carrier Delays

When carrier delays affect estimated delivery dates:

**Weather and natural disasters:**
- Delays caused by weather events, natural disasters, or government-ordered shutdowns are considered "force majeure" and do not trigger platform compensation
- The platform will update the estimated delivery date and notify the customer
- If the delay exceeds 14 days, the customer may cancel the order for a full refund

**Carrier operational issues:**
- Delays caused by carrier operational issues (vehicle breakdowns, staffing shortages, system outages) are monitored by the platform
- If a carrier consistently underperforms, the platform may switch carriers for affected regions
- Customers affected by carrier operational delays may receive a goodwill shipping credit for future orders

**Holiday season delays:**
- During peak shipping seasons (November 15 — January 5), delivery estimates include additional buffer time
- Orders with guaranteed delivery dates that miss the deadline during peak season may qualify for a shipping fee refund

## Section 5: Package Intercept and Redirect

Once a package is in transit with a carrier, the platform's ability to modify the delivery is limited:

1. **Package intercept** (stopping delivery and returning to sender): Available for UPS and FedEx shipments only, subject to a $15–$25 intercept fee charged by the carrier. The customer bears this cost unless the intercept is due to a platform error.
2. **Address redirect** (changing the delivery address): Available for UPS and FedEx, subject to carrier fees and limited to within the same delivery area. USPS packages cannot be redirected after handoff to the carrier.
3. **Hold for pickup** (delivering to a carrier location for customer pickup): Available for most carriers. The package will be held for 5–7 business days before being returned to sender.

Customers requesting package intercept or redirect are responsible for any carrier fees. The platform will inform the customer of the estimated cost before initiating the request.

## Section 6: Carrier Tracking Information

Tracking information is provided for all shipped orders:

1. Tracking numbers are shared via email within 24 hours of order shipment
2. Tracking updates may take 24–48 hours to appear in the carrier's system after the tracking number is generated
3. The platform provides a unified tracking page that consolidates information from all carriers
4. If tracking information has not updated for 72 hours after the tracking number was generated, the customer should contact support to verify the shipment status
5. "Pre-shipment" or "Label Created" status means the shipping label has been generated but the package has not yet been scanned by the carrier — this is normal for marketplace seller orders where the seller may have a processing delay

## Section 7: Return Shipping Carriers

For returns, the platform provides prepaid return labels through:
- USPS for standard-weight items (under 20 lbs)
- UPS for items over 20 lbs or oversized items
- FedEx for expedited returns or high-value items

Customers may also use their own carrier for returns, but:
1. The platform does not reimburse return shipping costs for customer-arranged returns unless the return is due to a platform error
2. The customer is responsible for items lost or damaged during customer-arranged return shipping
3. Customers who use their own carrier should provide the return tracking number to the platform for monitoring
