# Damaged and Defective Items — Detailed Procedures

**Document ID:** POL-DAM-015
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All damaged or defective item claims for first-party and marketplace orders

---

## Section 1: Damage Classification

Items received in a damaged condition are classified into the following categories to determine the appropriate resolution path:

### 1.1 Cosmetic Damage
Cosmetic damage refers to scratches, dents, discoloration, or other visual imperfections that do not affect the item's functionality. For cosmetic damage:
- The customer may return the item for a full refund within the standard return window
- Alternatively, the customer may choose to keep the item and receive a **partial refund of 15–25%** of the purchase price as compensation for the cosmetic imperfection
- The partial refund percentage is determined by the severity of the damage, as assessed by the support agent based on photographic evidence
- If the customer and agent cannot agree on the partial refund amount, the case may be escalated to a supervisor

### 1.2 Functional Damage
Functional damage renders the item unusable or significantly impairs its intended function. For functional damage:
- The customer is entitled to a full refund or free replacement
- The damage claim must be filed within 7 calendar days of delivery
- Photographic or video evidence of the functional damage is required
- For electronics, a description of the malfunction must be provided (e.g., "screen does not turn on," "motor makes grinding noise")

### 1.3 Packaging Damage Only
If the outer packaging is damaged but the item inside appears to be in good condition:
- The customer should inspect the item thoroughly before making a claim
- If the item is confirmed undamaged, no claim is necessary
- If the customer later discovers hidden damage (e.g., internal components dislodged), they may file a claim within the standard 7-day window
- If the damaged packaging reduces the item's value (e.g., a gift item with a damaged presentation box), the customer may request a partial refund of up to 10%

### 1.4 Concealed Damage
Concealed damage is damage that is not immediately visible upon delivery but is discovered upon opening the packaging:
- Concealed damage must be reported within **14 calendar days** of delivery (extended from the standard 7 days for visible damage)
- The customer must provide photographs of the item, the inner packaging, and the outer packaging
- Concealed damage claims may require a carrier inspection for high-value items

## Section 2: Defect vs. Damage Distinction

It is important to distinguish between damage (caused during shipping or handling) and defects (caused during manufacturing):

**Damage indicators:**
- Visible impact marks, dents, or cracks
- Broken or crushed packaging
- Water damage or staining
- Components separated or displaced from original positions

**Defect indicators:**
- Item does not function as described despite no physical damage
- Intermittent operation (works sometimes, fails at other times)
- Missing components that should have been included per the product specifications
- Design flaws that make the product unusable for its intended purpose

For damage: the claim is handled under the Returns and Refunds Policy (Section 3) and this policy.
For defects: the claim is handled under the Returns and Refunds Policy (Section 5) within 30 days, or the Warranty and Manufacturer Claims Policy after 30 days.

## Section 3: Resolution Options by Item Value

Resolution procedures depend on the item's purchase price:

### Items Under $25
- Refund issued immediately upon receipt of a damage/defect claim with photographic evidence
- No return of the item is required
- Replacement may be offered if the item is in stock
- Processing time: same day or next business day

### Items $25–$100
- Refund or replacement issued upon review of photographic evidence
- Return of the item is preferred but not required if the cost of return shipping exceeds 50% of the item value
- If return is not required, the customer may keep or dispose of the item
- Processing time: 1–2 business days

### Items $100–$500
- Refund or replacement issued upon review of photographic evidence AND receipt of the returned item
- A prepaid return shipping label will be provided
- The refund will be processed within 5 business days of receiving the returned item
- For urgent cases, a provisional refund may be issued before the returned item is received, at the supervisor's discretion

### Items Over $500
- A detailed damage report is required, including multiple photographs from different angles
- A carrier inspection may be required before the refund is processed
- The refund will be processed only after the returned item is received and inspected at the fulfillment center
- Processing time: 5–10 business days from receipt of returned item
- Supervisor approval is required for refunds on items over $500

## Section 4: Multiple Damaged Items in One Order

If a customer reports multiple damaged items in a single order:

1. Each item is evaluated individually — one item being damaged does not automatically qualify other items for a refund
2. If more than 50% of the items in an order are damaged, the entire order may be eligible for a full refund including shipping charges
3. The customer may choose to return only the damaged items and keep the undamaged ones
4. For large orders with multiple damaged items, consider escalating to the fulfillment operations team as this may indicate a systemic packaging or shipping issue

---

## Section 5: Repeated Damage Claims

If a customer files multiple damage claims across different orders:

1. First and second claims within a 6-month period: process normally per this policy
2. Third claim within a 6-month period: process the claim but flag the account for review
3. Fourth or subsequent claims: the claim should be reviewed by a supervisor before processing
4. Delivery address investigation: if multiple damage claims originate from the same delivery address, the fulfillment team should investigate potential carrier issues at that location (e.g., packages left in weather-exposed areas)

---

## Section 6: Damage Claim Denial

A damage claim may be denied in the following circumstances:

1. The claim was filed outside the applicable reporting window (7 days for visible damage, 14 days for concealed damage)
2. Photographic evidence does not support the damage claim
3. The damage appears to have been caused by the customer (e.g., drop marks inconsistent with shipping damage)
4. The item shows signs of extended use inconsistent with the reported timeline
5. The customer has a pattern of damage claims that has been flagged and reviewed

When denying a claim, the agent must:
- Clearly explain the reason for denial
- Cite the specific policy section that applies
- Inform the customer of their right to escalate the decision to a supervisor
- Never use accusatory language — maintain a professional, empathetic tone
