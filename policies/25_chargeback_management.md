# Chargeback and Payment Dispute Management

**Document ID:** POL-CHBK-025
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All payment disputes, chargebacks, and billing-related escalations

---

## Section 1: Understanding Chargebacks

A chargeback occurs when a customer contacts their bank or credit card issuer to dispute a charge on their statement. The bank then reverses the charge and initiates an investigation. Chargebacks are different from refund requests in several important ways:

1. **Chargebacks bypass the platform**: The customer goes directly to their bank, not to the platform's support team
2. **Chargebacks have financial consequences**: Each chargeback costs the platform a processing fee ($15–$30) in addition to the disputed amount
3. **Excessive chargebacks harm the platform**: High chargeback rates can result in increased payment processing fees or loss of the ability to accept certain payment methods
4. **Chargebacks take longer**: Bank investigations can take 30–90 days, whereas platform refunds are processed in 5–10 business days

For these reasons, the platform strongly encourages customers to contact support before filing a chargeback with their bank.

---

## Section 2: Pre-Chargeback Resolution

When a customer contacts support with a billing concern before filing a chargeback:

1. **Treat the inquiry with urgency** — resolving the issue before a chargeback is filed saves time and fees for both the customer and the platform
2. Review the customer's order and payment history
3. If the customer is entitled to a refund per policy, process it immediately with priority handling
4. If the customer's concern is legitimate but not covered by standard policy, consider a goodwill resolution — see Goodwill Gestures Policy
5. If the customer's concern is not legitimate (e.g., they received the item and are claiming they didn't), follow the standard dispute resolution process

When the dispute is resolved, **explicitly confirm with the customer that they will not proceed with a chargeback**. If a chargeback is filed after a refund has been issued, the platform may reverse the refund to avoid a double loss (see Section 4).

---

## Section 3: Chargeback Response Process

When a chargeback is filed:

1. **Notification**: The platform is notified by the payment processor, typically within 2–5 business days of the customer filing the chargeback
2. **Case assignment**: The chargeback is assigned to the billing team for investigation
3. **Evidence collection**: The billing team collects all relevant evidence:
   - Order confirmation and receipt
   - Delivery tracking and confirmation
   - Customer communication history
   - Photos of delivered items (if available)
   - Signed delivery confirmations
   - Return and refund history for the order
4. **Response submission**: The evidence package is submitted to the payment processor within the required response window (typically 7–14 business days)
5. **Decision**: The bank reviews the evidence and makes a determination:
   - **Customer wins**: The refund is finalized and the platform absorbs the loss plus processing fees
   - **Platform wins**: The charge is reinstated on the customer's account
   - **Pre-arbitration**: Either party may request further review, which involves an additional fee

---

## Section 4: Double Refund Prevention

If a customer receives both a platform refund AND a chargeback refund (also known as "double dipping"):

1. The platform will reverse the original refund or charge the customer's account for the duplicate amount
2. If the customer's payment method cannot be charged, the amount will be deducted from any store credit or future refunds owed to the customer
3. The customer will be notified of the reversal with a clear explanation
4. The customer's account will be flagged for chargeback monitoring

---

## Section 5: Legitimate Chargeback Reasons

The following are legitimate reasons for a chargeback that the platform should not contest:

1. **Unauthorized transaction**: The customer did not make the purchase (account was compromised)
2. **Item not received**: The customer did not receive their order and the platform failed to resolve the claim
3. **Item significantly not as described**: The customer received an item that is materially different from the listing and the platform refused to process a return
4. **Duplicate charge**: The customer was charged twice for the same order

For these cases, the platform should address the underlying issue and may choose not to contest the chargeback if the customer's claim is clearly valid.

---

## Section 6: Illegitimate Chargeback Reasons (Friendly Fraud)

"Friendly fraud" occurs when a customer files a chargeback despite having received the product or service. Common scenarios include:

1. Customer received the item but claims they didn't (especially with carrier delivery confirmation)
2. Customer used the product, returned it, and also filed a chargeback
3. Customer regrets the purchase and uses a chargeback instead of the return process
4. Family member made the purchase and the account holder claims it was unauthorized

For friendly fraud cases:
- The platform will vigorously contest the chargeback with all available evidence
- The customer's account will be flagged for fraud review
- If a pattern of friendly fraud is confirmed, the account may be restricted or terminated
- The platform may report confirmed friendly fraud to payment industry fraud databases

---

## Section 7: Chargeback Impact on Customer Account

Chargebacks have the following consequences for customer accounts:

| Number of Chargebacks (12 months) | Consequence |
|---|---|
| 1 | Informational flag — no action |
| 2 | Account placed on "Review Required" status |
| 3 | Account may be restricted to certain payment methods |
| 4+ | Account may be permanently restricted or terminated |

These consequences are applied by the fraud and risk management team, not by frontline support agents. Frontline agents should not mention these potential consequences to customers.

---

## Section 8: Customer Communication During Chargeback

When communicating with a customer who has filed a chargeback:

1. **Do not be accusatory**: Avoid suggesting the customer is acting in bad faith, even if friendly fraud is suspected
2. **Provide factual information**: Share delivery tracking, order details, and policy information
3. **Offer resolution**: If the chargeback is still pending, offer to resolve the issue directly (which is faster for the customer than the bank process)
4. **Do not make promises about the chargeback outcome**: The bank makes the final determination
5. **Document everything**: All communication with the customer during a chargeback investigation must be documented in the case record

## Section 9: Billing Error Investigation

When a customer reports a billing error (not a chargeback):

### 9.1 Common Billing Errors
1. **Incorrect amount charged**: The customer was charged a different amount than shown at checkout
2. **Currency conversion discrepancy**: For international orders, the charged amount in the customer's currency differs from the expected converted amount
3. **Tax calculation error**: Incorrect sales tax or VAT was applied
4. **Shipping fee discrepancy**: The shipping charge does not match what was shown at checkout
5. **Discount not applied**: A promotional discount or coupon was not reflected in the charge

### 9.2 Investigation Process
1. Compare the charged amount with the order total shown at checkout (including taxes and shipping)
2. Verify that all promotions, coupons, and discounts were correctly applied
3. For international orders, check the exchange rate applied by the payment processor
4. If an error is confirmed, issue a refund for the difference within 3–5 business days
5. If no error is found, provide a detailed breakdown of the charges to the customer

### 9.3 Recurring Billing Issues
If a customer reports recurring billing issues (e.g., subscription charged at the wrong amount):
1. Review the subscription terms and billing history
2. If the billing amount changed due to a price increase, verify that the customer was notified at least 30 days before the price change
3. If the customer was not properly notified, refund the difference and apply the correct rate going forward
4. For unresolved recurring billing disputes, escalate to the billing team
