# Lost Package Investigation Procedures

**Document ID:** POL-LPI-028
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All lost package claims and delivery investigations

---

## Section 1: Lost Package Definition and Thresholds

A package is classified as "lost" when it meets one or more of the following criteria established by the platform's operational standards:

### 1.1 Tracking Status Thresholds
1. **In-Transit stall**: Tracking status has not changed for 7 consecutive calendar days while the package is marked as "in transit" or "in transit to next facility"
2. **Missed delivery window**: The estimated delivery date has passed by 10 or more calendar days with no delivery scan
3. **Return-to-sender**: Tracking shows the package was returned to the fulfillment center without a delivery attempt, and the customer did not request the return
4. **Delivered to wrong address**: Carrier tracking shows delivery to an address that does not match the order's shipping address

### 1.2 Investigation Steps
When a lost package claim is filed, the following investigation procedure is followed:

**Step 1 — Tracking review (immediate):**
- Review the carrier tracking in full, including all scan events
- Check if the package was rerouted, held at a facility, or returned
- Check for any carrier service alerts (weather delays, facility closures)

**Step 2 — Carrier inquiry (within 24 hours):**
- Contact the carrier to request the current status of the package
- For packages showing a stalled status, request a trace or search
- Document the carrier's response in the case notes

**Step 3 — Customer communication (within 24 hours of claim):**
- Notify the customer that their claim is being investigated
- Provide a timeline for resolution (typically 3–5 business days)
- If the investigation is expected to take longer, provide an updated timeline

**Step 4 — Resolution (within 5 business days):**
- If the package is confirmed lost: process refund or reshipment per Section 2
- If the package is located: coordinate delivery with the carrier and update the customer
- If the investigation is inconclusive: process a goodwill refund for first-time claimants

---

## Section 2: Resolution Procedures

### 2.1 Confirmed Lost Package
When a package is confirmed lost by the carrier or determined lost through the investigation process:

**First-party orders:**
- The customer chooses between a full refund and a free reshipment
- If reshipment is chosen, the replacement order is shipped with expedited shipping at no additional cost
- If the original items are out of stock for reshipment, a full refund is issued
- The refund includes the original shipping charges paid by the customer

**Marketplace orders:**
- The customer should first contact the marketplace seller
- If the seller does not respond within 48 hours or refuses resolution, the Marketplace Guarantee applies
- Resolution under the Marketplace Guarantee follows the same terms as first-party lost package resolution

### 2.2 Inconclusive Investigation
When the investigation cannot definitively determine whether the package was delivered or lost:

- For first-time claimants with no history of delivery disputes: process a goodwill refund
- For customers with previous delivery dispute claims: escalate to the fraud review team
- Document the inconclusive finding in the case notes for future reference

## Section 3: High-Value Lost Package Claims

For lost package claims on orders valued at $300 or more:

1. **Enhanced investigation**: The carrier is required to provide a detailed trace report, including facility scans and GPS data (if available)
2. **Supervisor involvement**: A supervisor must review and approve the resolution before it is communicated to the customer
3. **Signature confirmation review**: Verify whether the order required signature confirmation at delivery — if signature was required but not obtained, the carrier is liable
4. **Police report**: For orders valued at $500 or more where theft is suspected, the customer may be asked to file a police report. This is not a requirement for processing the refund but strengthens the carrier liability claim.

---

## Section 4: International Lost Package Claims

International lost packages have additional complexity:

1. **Customs delays**: Packages held in customs are not considered lost. The platform will provide customs documentation to help the customer clear the package.
2. **International tracking gaps**: Tracking may not update during customs processing — allow up to 14 additional days before classifying as lost
3. **Carrier handoff issues**: International shipments may transfer between carriers (e.g., DHL to local postal service). Tracking may not transfer seamlessly — check both carrier systems.
4. **Resolution**: International lost package refunds include the purchase price and platform shipping charges. Customer-paid import duties and taxes are not refundable by the platform — the customer must apply for duty reimbursement with their local customs authority.
