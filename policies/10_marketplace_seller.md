# Marketplace Seller Policy

**Document ID:** POL-MKT-010
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All orders fulfilled by third-party marketplace sellers

---

## Section 1: Marketplace Seller Responsibilities

Marketplace sellers are independent businesses that use the platform to sell their products. They are responsible for:

1. Accurately describing their products in listings
2. Setting their own prices, shipping rates, and return policies
3. Fulfilling orders within their stated processing time
4. Responding to customer inquiries within 48 hours through the platform messaging system
5. Handling returns and refunds according to their stated policy
6. Complying with all applicable consumer protection laws

The platform acts as a marketplace facilitator and is not the seller of record for marketplace orders. However, the platform provides the **Marketplace Guarantee** to ensure customer protection.

---

## Section 2: Marketplace Guarantee

The Marketplace Guarantee provides customer protection when marketplace sellers fail to meet their obligations:

**Coverage:**
1. Full purchase price plus original shipping charges
2. Applies when the seller does not ship, ships a wrong item, or the item is significantly not as described
3. Applies when the seller does not respond to a customer dispute within 48 hours
4. Maximum coverage: $2,500 per claim, $10,000 per customer per year

**Exclusions:**
1. Buyer's remorse or change of mind
2. Minor variations from the listing (e.g., slight color differences due to photography)
3. Items confirmed delivered by the carrier
4. Disputes filed more than 60 days after the order date

---

## Section 3: Seller Return Policies

Each marketplace seller sets their own return policy, which must be displayed on their seller profile and product listings. Seller return policies must meet the following minimum standards:

1. Sellers must accept returns for items that are defective, damaged in transit, or not as described
2. Sellers must respond to return requests within 48 hours
3. Sellers cannot charge restocking fees exceeding 20% of the item price

If a seller's return policy is more restrictive than the platform's first-party policy, the following hierarchy applies:
- The seller's policy governs the return terms for the transaction
- **However**, if the customer's claim falls under the Marketplace Guarantee coverage, the platform's guarantee supersedes the seller's policy
- If there is a direct conflict between the seller's stated policy and the platform's Marketplace Guarantee, **the case must be escalated** for manual review rather than automatically applying either policy

---

## Section 4: Seller-Customer Disputes

When a customer has a dispute with a marketplace seller:

1. The customer contacts the seller through the platform messaging system
2. The seller has **48 hours** to respond with a proposed resolution
3. If the seller does not respond, the customer may escalate to platform support
4. Platform support will mediate the dispute and attempt to find a resolution acceptable to both parties
5. If mediation fails, the platform makes a final determination based on the evidence provided

During mediation:
- The platform may request additional evidence from both parties
- The platform will review the seller's return policy and the Marketplace Guarantee terms
- If the platform determines that the seller is at fault, the platform will issue a refund under the Marketplace Guarantee and charge the seller's account

---

## Section 5: Platform Guarantee vs. Seller Policy Conflicts

When the platform's Marketplace Guarantee and the seller's policy directly conflict:

**Example conflicts:**
1. Seller has a "no returns" policy, but the customer received a defective item → **Marketplace Guarantee applies** — defective items must be accepted for return
2. Seller accepts returns within 14 days, but the customer filed at day 25 → **Seller's policy governs** — unless the item is defective or not as described, in which case the Marketplace Guarantee 60-day window applies
3. Seller refuses a return for an item "not as described," but the customer provides photographic evidence of significant discrepancy → **Must be escalated** — platform support will review evidence and determine if the Marketplace Guarantee applies

**Critical rule:** When agent-resolvable logic cannot clearly determine which policy applies, the case **must be escalated to a supervisor**. The automated resolution system should never unilaterally override a seller's policy or deny a Marketplace Guarantee claim. Human review is required when policies conflict.
