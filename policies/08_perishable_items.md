# Perishable Items Policy

**Document ID:** POL-PERISH-008
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All orders containing perishable or temperature-sensitive items

---

## Section 1: Definition of Perishable Items

Perishable items include but are not limited to:
1. Fresh food, frozen food, refrigerated food
2. Fresh flowers and live plants
3. Baked goods without preservatives
4. Dairy products, meats, seafood
5. Chocolates and confections during warm weather months (May–September)
6. Temperature-sensitive health supplements (e.g., probiotics)

Items classified as "perishable" in the product catalog are subject to this policy, which provides specific exceptions to the standard Returns and Refunds Policy.

---

## Section 2: Return and Refund Exceptions for Perishables

Due to the nature of perishable goods, the following exceptions apply:

1. **No return required**: Perishable items that arrive damaged, spoiled, or melted are eligible for a full refund **without requiring return of the item**. The customer is not expected to ship back perishable goods.
2. **Reporting window**: Damage or quality issues with perishable items must be reported within **48 hours** of the confirmed delivery date. Reports made after 48 hours will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis but may be denied.
3. **Evidence required**: The customer must provide at least one photograph of the damaged or spoiled item. The photograph must clearly show the condition that makes the item unsuitable.
4. **Refund scope**: The refund covers the full purchase price of the perishable item(s) plus any applicable shipping charges that were incurred specifically for the perishable shipment.

---

## Section 3: Late Delivery of Perishables

If a perishable order arrives after the estimated delivery window and the items are spoiled or no longer usable:

1. The customer is entitled to a full refund for the perishable items
2. If the perishable items arrived late but are still in acceptable condition, no refund is issued but a **10% goodwill credit** may be offered for future purchases
3. Late delivery claims for perishables must be made within **24 hours** of the actual delivery

---

## Section 4: Temperature-Sensitive Packaging

First-party perishable orders are shipped in temperature-controlled packaging (insulated liners and gel packs). If the customer receives a perishable order and the temperature-controlled packaging appears compromised (melted gel packs, broken insulation):

1. The customer should photograph the packaging condition
2. Even if the food appears edible, a refund will be issued if the packaging integrity is compromised — the platform cannot guarantee food safety in these cases
3. The customer should not consume perishable items if the packaging was compromised

---

## Section 5: "Refund and Keep" Policy for Perishables

For perishable items where a refund is approved:

1. The customer **may keep the perishable item** — they are not required to return it
2. This policy exists because returned perishable items cannot be restocked or resold due to food safety regulations
3. The "refund and keep" policy applies **only to the perishable components** of an order. Non-perishable items in the same order must still be returned per the standard Returns and Refunds Policy to receive a refund
4. If a customer requests to keep non-perishable items while also receiving a refund, this request is **not covered** by the perishable exception and must be evaluated under the standard policy, which generally requires return of the item for a refund

**Important clarification**: The right to keep the item while receiving a refund is specific to perishable goods only. Any attempt to extend this benefit to non-perishable items constitutes a non-standard request that cannot be approved without supervisor authorization.
