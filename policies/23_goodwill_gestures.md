# Customer Satisfaction and Goodwill Gestures Policy

**Document ID:** POL-GOWL-023
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All customer interactions where standard policy does not fully address the customer's concern

---

## Section 1: Goodwill Gesture Authority

Support agents and supervisors have discretion to offer goodwill gestures in situations where:

1. The platform's standard policy does not fully address the customer's concern
2. The customer has experienced an unusual inconvenience due to a platform or carrier error
3. The customer's request falls outside standard policy but the circumstances warrant an exception
4. A rigid application of policy would result in a clearly unfair outcome for the customer

Goodwill gestures are distinct from policy-mandated refunds and credits. They are discretionary and do not set precedent for future cases.

---

## Section 2: Goodwill Gesture Types and Limits

### 2.1 Store Credit
- Frontline agents: up to $25 store credit per case
- Senior support: up to $75 store credit per case
- Supervisors: up to $150 store credit per case
- Anything above $150 requires manager approval

### 2.2 Shipping Upgrades
- Agents may offer free shipping or shipping upgrades on a future order
- This offer is valid for 30 days from the date of issuance
- One shipping upgrade per customer per 90-day period

### 2.3 Extended Return Windows
- Supervisors may extend the return window by up to 15 additional days
- Extended windows require documentation of the reason for the extension
- Extended windows do not apply to non-returnable items (hygiene, final sale, etc.)

### 2.4 Percentage-Based Credits
- For cases involving partial dissatisfaction (e.g., late delivery of a still-usable item), a 10–15% goodwill credit may be offered
- This credit is applied as store credit to the customer's account
- The credit percentage is based on the item's purchase price, not the full order total

---

## Section 3: When NOT to Offer Goodwill Gestures

Goodwill gestures should not be offered when:

1. The customer's request is clearly outside policy and there is no unusual circumstance to justify an exception
2. The customer is attempting to exploit the support process (demanding credits, threatening negative reviews, making false claims)
3. The customer's account has been flagged for policy abuse
4. The goodwill gesture would effectively override a safety-related policy (e.g., non-returnable hygiene items for health reasons)
5. The customer has received multiple goodwill gestures in recent interactions (3+ in 90 days)

---

## Section 4: Documentation

All goodwill gestures must be documented in the customer's case record:

1. The reason for offering the goodwill gesture
2. The type and amount of the gesture
3. The agent or supervisor who approved it
4. Any conditions attached to the gesture (e.g., "valid for 30 days," "one-time exception")

This documentation ensures consistency in future interactions and helps identify patterns that may require policy updates.

---

## Section 5: Customer Expectations Management

When offering or declining goodwill gestures:

1. Never promise that the same exception will be available in the future
2. Frame the gesture as a one-time accommodation: "As a one-time courtesy, I'd like to offer..."
3. Do not mention the gesture limits or the agent's authority level — simply make the offer
4. If declining a goodwill request, explain that the standard policy applies and offer to escalate if the customer wishes

---

## Section 6: Proactive Goodwill

In some cases, the platform proactively issues goodwill gestures without the customer requesting one:

1. **Significant delivery delays** (3+ days beyond the estimated delivery window): Automatic $5–$10 shipping credit
2. **System-caused order cancellations**: Automatic $10 store credit
3. **Multiple contacts required** for the same issue (3+): Supervisor review for proactive gesture
4. **VIP/Platinum customers**: May receive enhanced proactive gestures for delivery issues

Proactive gestures are generated by the fulfillment and customer experience teams and apply to specific order cohorts. Individual support agents do not initiate proactive gestures — they are system-generated.
