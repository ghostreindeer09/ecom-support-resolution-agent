# Final Sale and Clearance Policy

**Document ID:** POL-FS-014
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All items marked as Final Sale or purchased during clearance events

---

## Section 1: Final Sale Definition

Items designated as "Final Sale" are products offered at a significant discount with the understanding that they are non-returnable and non-refundable. Final Sale designation:

1. Is clearly marked on the product page with a "Final Sale" badge
2. Is confirmed in the cart and during checkout
3. Is stated in the order confirmation email
4. Cannot be removed or overridden by customer support agents

---

## Section 2: Return Restrictions

Final Sale items are subject to a **strict no-return, no-refund policy** regardless of the reason for return:

1. Change of mind → No return
2. Item doesn't fit → No return or exchange
3. Item not as expected → No return (unless item is materially different from the listing, which constitutes fraud by the seller)
4. Found a better price → No return

The only exceptions to the Final Sale no-return policy are:

1. **Item received is materially different from the listing** (wrong item, completely different product) → Return and refund permitted
2. **Item is defective or damaged in transit** → Return and refund permitted with evidence per the Returns and Refunds Policy
3. **Regional consumer protection law provides a mandatory return right** that supersedes the Final Sale designation → Must be escalated to legal compliance

---

## Section 3: Clearance Events

During platform-wide clearance events:

1. All clearance items are marked as Final Sale unless otherwise specified
2. Clearance pricing is available while supplies last
3. No price adjustments will be made after purchase, even if the clearance price drops further
4. Clearance items may have limited warranty coverage — check the product page for specific warranty terms

---

## Section 4: Customer Communication

When a customer contacts support about a Final Sale item:

1. Verify that the item was indeed marked as Final Sale by checking the order details
2. If confirmed as Final Sale, explain the policy clearly and empathetically
3. If the customer cites a consumer protection law, escalate to legal compliance
4. If the customer received a damaged or defective Final Sale item, process normally per the Returns and Refunds Policy — the Final Sale restriction does not apply to defective or damaged items
5. Never use the phrase "you agreed to this" — instead, acknowledge the frustration and explain the policy in neutral terms
