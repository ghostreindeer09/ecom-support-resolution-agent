# Warranty and Manufacturer Claims Policy

**Document ID:** POL-WAR-013
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All items with manufacturer warranties sold through the platform

---

## Section 1: Platform vs. Manufacturer Warranty

The platform provides a **30-day return window** for defective items (see Returns and Refunds Policy, Section 5). After the 30-day window closes, the platform's responsibility for defects ends, and the customer should pursue the manufacturer's warranty.

Key distinctions:
1. **Within 30 days of delivery**: The platform handles the defect claim directly — refund, replacement, or repair at the customer's choice
2. **After 30 days, within manufacturer warranty period**: The customer must contact the manufacturer directly. The platform can provide purchase documentation (receipt, order confirmation) to support the manufacturer warranty claim
3. **After manufacturer warranty expires**: No warranty coverage exists. The customer may pursue state-specific implied warranty rights if applicable

---

## Section 2: Platform Support for Manufacturer Claims

While the platform does not process manufacturer warranty claims, support agents can assist customers by:

1. Providing a copy of the purchase receipt or order confirmation
2. Providing the manufacturer's contact information (if available in the product listing)
3. Confirming the order date and delivery date to help the customer determine if the item is still within the manufacturer's warranty period
4. Confirming the item's model number and serial number (if recorded in the order system)

---

## Section 3: Extended Warranty Programs

The platform offers optional extended warranty plans through third-party warranty providers. These plans:

1. Are separate from both the platform's return policy and the manufacturer's warranty
2. Are administered by the third-party warranty provider, not the platform
3. Have their own terms, conditions, and claims processes
4. Support agents should direct extended warranty claims to the warranty provider
5. The platform does not process refunds or claims for extended warranty plans

---

## Section 4: Items That Fail Shortly After the Return Window

If an item develops a defect shortly after the 30-day return window (e.g., within 31–45 days):

1. The platform **does not** offer a grace period beyond the stated 30-day return window
2. The customer should be directed to the manufacturer's warranty process
3. If the item category qualifies for an implied warranty under the customer's state or regional law, the case should be escalated to legal compliance for review
4. Goodwill exceptions may be made at a supervisor's discretion for loyal customers or clearly defective items, but this is not a standard policy and should not be promised

---

## Section 5: Warranty Fraud

If a customer claims a manufacturing defect but evidence suggests misuse or intentional damage:

1. The platform may deny the claim based on photographic or video evidence showing misuse
2. If the evidence is unclear, the case should be escalated for review
3. Patterns of warranty claims from the same customer (3+ claims in 12 months) should be flagged for potential warranty fraud
