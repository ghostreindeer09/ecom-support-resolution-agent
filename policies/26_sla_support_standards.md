# Platform Service Level Agreements and Support Standards

**Document ID:** POL-SLA-026
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All customer support interactions and service commitments

---

## Section 1: Response Time Commitments

The platform commits to the following response times for customer support inquiries:

### 1.1 Initial Response Times
| Channel | Standard Response | Priority Response |
|---|---|---|
| Email support | Within 24 hours | Within 4 hours |
| Live chat | Within 5 minutes | Within 2 minutes |
| Phone support | Within 3 minutes hold time | Within 1 minute |
| Social media | Within 4 hours | Within 1 hour |
| Self-service portal | Automated acknowledgment immediately | N/A |

Priority response times apply to:
- Orders marked as gifts with upcoming event dates
- Perishable item issues
- Orders valued above $500
- Customers with Gold or Platinum loyalty tier status
- Cases flagged as URGENT or HIGH priority

### 1.2 Resolution Time Targets
| Case Type | Target Resolution Time |
|---|---|
| Simple refund (standard policy) | Same day or next business day |
| Damaged/defective item claim | 1–3 business days |
| Lost package claim | 2–5 business days |
| Payment dispute | 3–5 business days |
| Marketplace seller dispute | 5–10 business days (includes seller response window) |
| Escalated cases | 1–5 business days depending on complexity |
| Legal/compliance review | 3–10 business days |
| Fraud investigation | 1–5 business days |

These are targets, not guarantees. Complex cases may require additional time, and the customer should be kept informed of progress.

## Section 2: Support Channel Capabilities

Not all support channels have the same capabilities:

### 2.1 Self-Service Portal
Customers can independently:
- Initiate returns and print return labels
- Track orders and shipments
- Cancel orders (if not yet shipped)
- Request basic refunds for eligible items
- Update account information
- View order history and past support interactions

### 2.2 Live Chat and Phone
Support agents can:
- Process refunds and replacements
- Issue store credits and goodwill gestures (within their authority limits)
- Escalate cases to specialist teams
- Access order details, tracking information, and account notes
- Provide real-time troubleshooting for product or service issues

### 2.3 Email Support
Email agents can:
- Handle all actions available to live chat/phone agents
- Process complex cases that require research and investigation
- Provide detailed written explanations of policy decisions
- Attach documents and evidence to the case record

### 2.4 Specialist Teams (Escalation)
Specialist teams (legal, fraud, billing, senior support) can:
- Override standard policy within their authority
- Make exceptions that exceed frontline agent limits
- Coordinate with external parties (carriers, sellers, manufacturers, banks)
- Authorize refunds above standard limits

## Section 3: Quality Assurance Standards

All customer support interactions are subject to quality assurance review:

### 3.1 Accuracy Standards
- **Policy accuracy**: Agents must cite the correct policy and apply it accurately to the customer's situation. Incorrect policy application is a critical quality failure.
- **Information accuracy**: All information provided to the customer (timelines, eligibility, next steps) must be factually correct.
- **Calculation accuracy**: Refund amounts, partial refunds, restocking fees, and credits must be calculated correctly.

### 3.2 Communication Standards
- Follow the Customer Communication Standards Policy (POL-COMM-016) for tone, structure, and language requirements
- Use the customer's preferred language if multilingual support is available
- Maintain professionalism even when the customer is upset or uncooperative
- Never argue with the customer — de-escalate and offer options

### 3.3 Documentation Standards
- All customer interactions must be documented in the case management system
- Case notes must include: customer's issue, information gathering performed, policy applied, decision made, and outcome communicated
- Sensitive information (full card numbers, SSN, etc.) must never be recorded in case notes
- Case notes should be objective and factual — no editorializing about the customer

## Section 4: Service Recovery

When the platform fails to meet its service commitments (e.g., response time missed, incorrect resolution provided):

### 4.1 Identifying Service Failures
1. Response time exceeded the committed window
2. Incorrect policy applied to a case (discovered upon review)
3. Customer had to contact support multiple times for the same unresolved issue
4. Customer was provided incorrect information that led to negative consequences
5. Escalation was delayed or lost in the queue

### 4.2 Recovery Actions
1. **Immediate correction**: Fix the original issue as the first priority
2. **Acknowledgment**: Apologize for the service failure specifically and sincerely
3. **Compensation**: Offer a goodwill gesture appropriate to the severity:
   - Missed response time: $5 shipping credit
   - Multiple contacts required (3+): $10–$25 store credit
   - Incorrect resolution: Full correction plus $10–$25 store credit
   - Significant impact on the customer: Supervisor review for enhanced compensation
4. **Root cause analysis**: Document the failure for quality improvement review

## Section 5: Continuous Improvement

The platform uses the following data to continuously improve support quality:

1. **Customer satisfaction surveys** (CSAT): Sent after each support interaction
2. **Net Promoter Score** (NPS): Quarterly measurement of overall customer satisfaction
3. **First Contact Resolution** (FCR) rate: Percentage of cases resolved in a single interaction
4. **Average handling time** (AHT): Time from first contact to case closure
5. **Escalation rate**: Percentage of cases requiring escalation from frontline to specialist
6. **Quality audit scores**: Regular audits of support interactions against quality standards

Target metrics:
- CSAT: ≥ 90%
- FCR: ≥ 75%
- Average resolution time: ≤ 2 business days for standard cases
- Quality audit score: ≥ 95%
- Escalation rate: ≤ 15%

## Section 6: Multichannel Consistency

Customers may contact support through multiple channels for the same issue. The platform ensures consistency through:

1. **Unified case management**: All channels access the same case management system
2. **Case history visibility**: Agents can see all previous interactions regardless of channel
3. **Cross-channel handoff**: If a customer starts on chat and needs to continue via email, the case transfers seamlessly
4. **Consistent policy application**: The same policies apply regardless of the support channel
5. **No channel advantage**: Customers should not receive different outcomes based on which channel they use

## Section 7: Operating Hours and Holiday Coverage

### 7.1 Standard Operating Hours
- **Live chat and phone**: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM ET; Saturday–Sunday, 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM ET
- **Email support**: Monitored 24/7, with responses within the committed SLA windows
- **Self-service portal**: Available 24/7
- **Social media**: Monitored during business hours; urgent issues (safety, fraud) monitored after hours

### 7.2 Holiday Coverage
- Reduced staffing on US federal holidays — response times may be extended by 1 business day
- Extended operating hours during the period from Black Friday through December 31
- Emergency support (fraud alerts, safety issues, chargeback notifications) available 24/7/365

### 7.3 Maintenance Windows
- Planned system maintenance occurs weekly between 2:00 AM – 4:00 AM ET on Tuesdays
- During maintenance windows:
  - Self-service portal may be unavailable
  - Live chat and phone support remain available
  - Email submissions are queued and processed after maintenance completes
  - Customers are notified of planned maintenance 48 hours in advance via banner on the website

## Section 8: Accessibility

The platform is committed to accessible customer support:

1. **TTY/TDD support**: Available for hearing-impaired customers
2. **Screen reader compatibility**: Self-service portal is designed to be compatible with common screen readers
3. **Large print**: Written communications can be provided in large print upon request
4. **Language support**: Support is available in English, Spanish, and French. Additional languages available through translation services with a 24-hour response time.
5. **Cognitive accessibility**: Support agents are trained to communicate clearly, avoid jargon, and adjust their communication style for customers who need additional assistance

## Section 9: Data Retention for Support Records

Customer support records are retained as follows:

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Chat transcripts | 3 years |
| Email correspondence | 5 years |
| Phone call recordings | 2 years |
| Case notes and resolution records | 7 years |
| Escalation packages | 7 years |
| Complaint records | 7 years |
| Internal quality audits | 3 years |

All records are stored in compliance with applicable data protection regulations. Customers may request deletion of their support records subject to legal retention requirements (data protection obligations may require retention of certain records regardless of the customer's deletion request).

After the retention period expires, records are automatically and irreversibly deleted from all systems, including backups.
