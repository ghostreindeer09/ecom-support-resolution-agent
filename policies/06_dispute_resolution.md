# Dispute Resolution Policy

**Document ID:** POL-DISP-006
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All customer disputes involving orders, deliveries, and transactions

---

## Section 1: Dispute Categories

Customer disputes are categorized as follows:

1. **Item not received (INR)**: The customer did not receive their order
2. **Item not as described (INAD)**: The customer received an item that differs from the product listing
3. **Damaged in transit**: The item was damaged during shipping
4. **Missing items**: One or more items from the order are missing
5. **Unauthorized transaction**: The customer did not authorize the purchase
6. **Quality dispute**: The customer believes the item quality does not match descriptions or expectations

Each dispute category has a specific resolution path defined in this policy and may reference other policy documents.

---

## Section 2: Dispute Filing Window

Customers must file a dispute within the following windows:

| Dispute Type | Filing Window |
|---|---|
| Item not received | 60 days from order date |
| Item not as described | 30 days from delivery date |
| Damaged in transit | 7 days from delivery date |
| Missing items | 14 days from delivery date |
| Unauthorized transaction | 90 days from transaction date |
| Quality dispute | 30 days from delivery date |

Disputes filed outside these windows will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis but may be denied. Extenuating circumstances (illness, travel, natural disaster) may be considered for late filings.

---

## Section 3: Evidence Requirements

To process a dispute, the following evidence may be required:

**Item not received:**
- Order number and delivery tracking information
- Confirmation that the customer has checked with household members and neighbors
- For orders showing carrier delivery confirmation: signed statement of non-receipt

**Item not as described:**
- Photographs showing the discrepancy between the listing and the received item
- Screenshot of the original product listing for comparison
- Description of how the item differs from the listing

**Damaged in transit:**
- Photographs of the damaged item and packaging
- Description of the damage
- Retention of packaging for potential carrier inspection

**Missing items:**
- Order number showing all items purchased
- Photograph of the items received (to confirm which are missing)
- Packing slip (if included in the shipment)

## Section 4: Dispute Resolution Process

1. **Filing**: Customer files a dispute through support or the self-service portal
2. **Acknowledgment**: The platform acknowledges the dispute within 24 hours
3. **Investigation**: The support team reviews the evidence and order records (1–3 business days)
4. **Resolution**: One of the following outcomes:
   - **Refund**: Full or partial refund issued
   - **Replacement**: Replacement item shipped
   - **Denial**: Dispute denied with explanation
   - **Escalation**: Complex cases are escalated to senior support or management

For marketplace disputes, the process includes an additional mediation step between the customer and seller before platform resolution.

---

## Section 5: Customer Dispute Window

Customers have the right to contest a delivery or transaction through the platform's dispute resolution process. When a customer claims they did not receive a package that the carrier shows as delivered:

1. The customer may file a formal delivery dispute within **5 business days** of the carrier's delivery confirmation date
2. The platform will investigate by reviewing carrier delivery data, GPS coordinates (if available), and any delivery photographs
3. During the investigation period (up to 5 business days), no refund decision will be made
4. If the investigation is inconclusive, the platform will issue a refund as a goodwill gesture for first-time disputes
5. If the investigation shows clear evidence of delivery (GPS-verified, photo evidence, signature), the dispute may be denied

For disputes involving carrier signature confirmation that the customer denies signing:
- The case requires escalation to the fraud investigation team
- The customer will be asked to file a police report for potential package theft
- Resolution will be handled by a senior support agent, not through automated channels

---

## Section 6: Marketplace Dispute Resolution

For disputes involving marketplace seller orders:

1. The customer must first contact the marketplace seller through the platform's messaging system
2. The seller has **48 hours** to respond and offer a resolution
3. If the seller does not respond or the customer is not satisfied with the proposed resolution, the customer may escalate the dispute to the platform
4. The platform will review both parties' evidence and make a determination
5. If the platform finds in the customer's favor, the **Marketplace Guarantee** will cover the resolution (refund or replacement)
6. If the platform finds in the seller's favor, the dispute is closed and the customer may pursue resolution through external channels

Marketplace sellers who accumulate excessive disputes (more than 5% dispute rate over 90 days) will be subject to performance review and potential suspension from the platform.

---

## Section 7: Escalation Criteria

The following disputes must be escalated to senior support or management:

1. Disputes involving orders valued above $500
2. Disputes where the customer references specific consumer protection laws
3. Disputes involving potential fraud (either by the customer or an unauthorized third party)
4. Disputes that have not been resolved within 10 business days
5. Disputes where the platform's standard policy conflicts with regional consumer protection regulations
6. Repeated disputes from the same customer (3+ in 90 days)
7. Disputes involving marketplace sellers who are unresponsive or uncooperative
