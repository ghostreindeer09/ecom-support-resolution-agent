# Regional Consumer Protection Compliance

**Document ID:** POL-REG-011
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** Orders shipped to regions with specific consumer protection laws

---

## Section 1: Overview

The platform operates in multiple jurisdictions, each with different consumer protection regulations. When a customer references a regional consumer protection law, or when the shipping region triggers a compliance flag, the following policy applies.

**Important**: Platform customer support agents and automated systems are **NOT authorized to interpret or apply regional consumer protection laws**. When a regional law is referenced or when a regional compliance flag is triggered, the case **must be escalated** to the legal compliance team for review.

---

## Section 2: European Union (EU) Consumer Rights

EU customers are protected by the EU Consumer Rights Directive, which provides:

1. **14-day cooling-off period**: EU customers have the right to withdraw from a distance purchase within 14 calendar days of receiving the goods, without giving any reason. This is a minimum right that cannot be waived by the platform's terms of service.
2. **Refund within 14 days of withdrawal**: The platform must refund the customer within 14 calendar days of the withdrawal notification
3. **Return shipping costs**: The customer bears the cost of return shipping unless the platform agreed to bear those costs or failed to inform the customer that they would bear the costs
4. **Exceptions**: The cooling-off period does not apply to perishable goods, sealed goods that have been opened and cannot be returned for hygiene reasons, and goods made to the customer's specifications

**Platform policy for EU customers:**
- The platform's 30-day return window exceeds the EU 14-day minimum, so no conflict exists for returns within 30 days
- **However**, if a customer is claiming rights under EU law that extend beyond or conflict with the platform's standard policy (e.g., claiming a right to return after the platform's 30-day window but within a timeframe the customer believes is covered by EU law), the case **must be escalated to legal compliance**
- Customer support agents should not make determinations about EU law compliance — this requires legal review

## Section 3: United Kingdom (UK) Consumer Rights

UK customers are protected by the Consumer Rights Act 2015:

1. **30-day right to reject**: UK customers can reject faulty goods within 30 days for a full refund
2. **6-month reverse burden of proof**: Within the first 6 months, if a fault appears, it is presumed the fault existed at the time of delivery (the burden is on the seller to prove otherwise)
3. **Repair or replacement right**: After 30 days, the customer has the right to a repair or replacement. If repair/replacement fails, they are entitled to a refund.

Platform policy aligns with UK consumer rights for the 30-day window. For claims made between 30 days and 6 months citing UK consumer law, the case must be escalated to legal compliance.

## Section 4: United States — State-Specific Laws

While US federal law does not mandate a right of return, several states have specific consumer protection laws:

**California:**
- California Civil Code §1723 requires that return policies be clearly displayed at the point of sale
- If no return policy is clearly posted, customers have **30 days** to return merchandise for a full refund
- California's Lemon Law may apply to defective electronics — cases citing this law must be escalated
- Consumers are protected against undisclosed fees and charges under California's consumer protection statutes

**New York:**
- New York General Business Law §218-a requires that refund policies be posted at the point of sale
- If not posted, customers have **30 days** to return merchandise

**Other states:**
- Most states have implied warranty protections that may extend beyond the platform's standard return window
- Any customer referencing a specific state law should have their case escalated to legal compliance

---

## Section 5: Canadian Consumer Protection

Canadian consumer protection laws vary by province:

1. Most provinces provide a "cooling-off period" of 10–15 days for distance sales
2. Implied warranty provisions may extend beyond the platform's standard return window
3. Cases involving Canadian provincial law should be escalated

---

## Section 6: Regional Policy Conflict Resolution

When a regional consumer protection law conflicts with the platform's standard policy:

1. **The case must be escalated** to the legal compliance team — it should never be resolved by frontline support or automated systems
2. Do not tell the customer that their regional law does or does not apply — this is a legal determination
3. Acknowledge the customer's concern and inform them that their case is being reviewed by a specialist team
4. Provide a timeline: cases involving regional law review are typically resolved within **3–5 business days**
5. Do not deny a claim based solely on platform policy when the customer has referenced a regional law that may supersede it

**Priority**: Cases involving regional law conflicts are classified as **URGENT** for internal escalation purposes, as failure to comply with consumer protection laws may expose the platform to legal liability.
