# Special Circumstances and Exception Handling

**Document ID:** POL-SPEC-020
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2026
**Applies To:** All orders and support interactions under special circumstances

---

## Section 1: Force Majeure Events

During events beyond the platform's control (natural disasters, pandemics, civil unrest, significant weather events, major carrier network outages):

1. Shipping estimates may be extended without prior notice
2. Return windows may be extended by the duration of the force majeure event plus 7 calendar days
3. Cancellation requests for delayed orders will be processed as a priority
4. Customers in affected areas will receive proactive notification of potential delays
5. Perishable items that cannot be delivered during a force majeure event will receive automatic refunds

The platform will publish force majeure notices on the website and through email communication to affected customers. Customer support agents should check for active force majeure notices before denying time-sensitive claims.

---

## Section 2: Bulk and Corporate Orders

Orders placed in bulk quantities (10+ units of the same item) or through the corporate purchasing program are subject to modified terms:

1. **Return limitations**: Bulk orders may be subject to a 10% restocking fee regardless of the item category, and returns of partial quantities from a bulk order may not be accepted
2. **Special pricing**: Corporate and bulk pricing are contractual and confidential — support agents should not reference specific pricing terms
3. **Dedicated support**: Bulk and corporate customers are served by the business accounts team (business@platform.com)
4. **Standard consumer protection**: Despite the modified terms, bulk buyers who are individual consumers (not businesses) retain all standard consumer protection rights

---

## Section 3: Gift Orders

When the recipient of a gift order contacts support (they received the item as a gift, not the purchaser):

1. **Returns**: Gift recipients may return items using the gift receipt or order confirmation provided by the purchaser
2. **Refund destination**: Refunds for gift returns are issued as **store credit** to the gift recipient's account, not as a refund to the original purchaser's payment method
3. **Gift receipts**: If the gift recipient does not have the gift receipt, they should contact the purchaser to obtain the order information
4. **Price visibility**: The gift receipt does not show the purchase price — if a refund is needed, it is based on the current selling price of the item, not the price paid by the purchaser

---

## Section 4: Pre-Order Items

Items available for pre-order (not yet released or not yet in stock) are subject to the following:

1. **Cancellation**: Pre-orders may be cancelled at any time before the item ships for a full refund
2. **Price guarantee**: If the item's price drops between the pre-order date and the ship date, the customer will be charged the lower price
3. **Delay communication**: If the pre-order item's release date is delayed, the customer will be notified and given the option to cancel
4. **Return window**: The standard 30-day return window begins on the delivery date, not the pre-order placement date

---

## Section 5: Out-of-Stock Resolution

When an ordered item goes out of stock after the order is placed:

1. The customer will be notified within 24 hours of the out-of-stock determination
2. Options offered to the customer:
   - Wait for restock (estimated timeline provided if available)
   - Substitute with a comparable item at the same or lower price
   - Cancel for a full refund
3. If no restock timeline is available, the order will be automatically cancelled after 7 business days with a full refund
4. One-time goodwill credit of $5–$10 may be offered for the inconvenience

---

## Section 6: Incorrect Pricing

If an item is listed at an incorrect price due to a system error, the platform reserves the right to:

1. Cancel orders placed at the incorrect price with a full refund
2. Contact the customer to offer the item at the correct price
3. If the price difference is minor (within 10% of the correct price), the platform may honor the listed price as a goodwill gesture

Pricing errors must be verified by the merchandising team before any order cancellations are processed. Customer support agents should not unilaterally declare a price as "incorrect" — this determination requires verification.

---

## Section 7: Environmental and Sustainability Returns

The platform has sustainability initiatives that affect certain return processes:

1. **Items too costly to return**: For low-value items where the cost of return shipping exceeds the item's value, the customer may be offered a refund without return. The customer is encouraged to either keep the item or donate it to a local charity rather than discard it.
2. **Oversized items**: For large items (furniture, appliances) where return shipping has a significant carbon footprint, local donation or recycling alternatives may be offered alongside the standard return process.
3. **Packaging waste**: Customers are encouraged to reuse packaging materials when making returns. The platform provides digital return labels (not physical inserts) to reduce waste.

These sustainability options are offered as alternatives, not requirements. The customer always retains the right to use the standard return process.

---

## Section 8: Emergency and Compassion Cases

In extraordinary circumstances (bereavement, medical emergency, house fire, natural disaster affecting the customer), support agents and supervisors have discretion to:

1. Extend return windows beyond the standard period
2. Waive restocking fees
3. Expedite refund processing
4. Issue goodwill credits beyond normal limits
5. Process exceptions to non-returnable item policies on a case-by-case basis

These compassion exceptions require supervisor approval and are documented separately from standard policy applications. They do not set precedent for future cases and are not available as a general customer benefit.

---

## Section 9: Third-Party Warranty Provider Claims

When a customer contacts the platform about a product covered by a third-party extended warranty:

1. Verify that the customer purchased the extended warranty through the platform
2. Provide the warranty provider's contact information and claim process
3. The platform does not process extended warranty claims — the customer must work directly with the warranty provider
4. If the customer has difficulty reaching the warranty provider, the platform may facilitate contact but cannot authorize claim payments
5. If the warranty provider is unresponsive for 14+ days, the customer may escalate through the platform for mediation assistance

---

## Section 10: Cross-Border Returns

For items purchased on the platform's international storefronts and returned across borders:

1. Customs duties paid on the original purchase are not refundable by the platform — the customer must apply for duty reimbursement with their local customs authority
2. Return shipping costs for cross-border returns are the customer's responsibility unless the return is due to a platform error
3. Items must comply with export regulations when being returned — certain items (electronics with encryption, regulated goods) may have restrictions on cross-border return shipping
4. Allow additional processing time (5–10 extra business days) for cross-border returns due to customs clearance of the returned package
5. Refunds for cross-border returns will be issued in the original transaction currency. Exchange rate differences between the purchase date and refund date are not compensated
